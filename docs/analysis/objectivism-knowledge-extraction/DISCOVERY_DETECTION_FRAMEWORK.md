# Discovery Detection Framework (DDF)
## Applying Objectivist Epistemology to Human-AI Coding Session Analysis

*Developed: 2026-02-20*
*Status: Active research — foundational document for Phases 14, 15, 16*

---

## Overview

The Discovery Detection Framework (DDF) is a method for identifying, classifying, and measuring moments of genuine conceptual breakthrough in human-AI coding interactions. It is grounded in Harry Binswanger's epistemology (*How We Know*), Leonard Peikoff's Spiral Theory of Knowledge, the Genus Method, and the Shuttling psycho-epistemological habit.

The core claim: **A "light bulb" moment is not mystical. It is a visible shift in the cognitive geometry of the interaction** — detectable by a third-person observer as a structural change in the human's output, not merely its content.

The role of the detector is called the **Epistemological Flame-Spotter**, drawn from John Galt's self-description in *Atlas Shrugged*: watching for the bright flares of rational identification in a session.

---

## The Seven Detection Markers (Ordered Hierarchy)

The markers form a hierarchy — not just conceptually, but in first-encounter order. Later markers retroactively illuminate earlier instances (Spiral Theory). Each marker is also a detection of **successful shuttling** (the psycho-epistemological motion between concrete and principle).

### Level 1 — Algebra of the Prompt
**Theory:** Binswanger's "Algebra of Cognition" — a concept stands for any number of units, just as a variable stands for any number of values.

**Detection signal:** The human moves from hard-coded concretes to abstract variables.
- *Before:* "Make this button blue"
- *After:* "Define a `PrimaryActionTheme` that governs every interactive element"

**Why it matters:** This is the atomic unit of conceptual ascent. It appears in any session and is the cleanest binary signal — either the human is issuing constants or variables.

**Shuttling connection:** The upward path of cognition. The question is whether the abstraction was reached by observing a flood of concretes or by reorganizing vocabulary.

---

### Level 2 — Piles to Files (CCD/Axis Shift)
**Theory:** Binswanger's "file folder" analogy — out-of-focus thinking deals with piles; focused innovation creates a file that integrates them. Similarity is measurement-proximity along an axis.

**Detection signal:** The human identifies a Conceptual Common Denominator (CCD) linking disparate modules the agent treated as unrelated.
- *Before:* Five distinct instructions for five functions
- *After:* "Treat all these functions as variants of [New Concept X]"

**The axis reveal:** "The button and the database are both reflecting the 'Sync-Status' axis — rewrite the agent's logic to prioritize that measurement."

**Unit economy:** Complexity drops from N problems to 1. The "Crow" count (Miller's cognitive limit) drops sharply.

---

### Level 3 — Causal Isolation (Fundamentality)
**Theory:** A fundamental characteristic is the one that "explains the greatest number of others."

**Detection signal:** The human stops tweaking symptoms and identifies the root cause — a single variable or class that, if changed, refactors the entire system's behavior.

**Observable:** Sharp drop in "instructional noise." The human becomes ruthlessly precise. They have isolated the fundamental that causes all derivative bugs.

**Shuttling connection:** The deepest point of the upward shuttle — the most abstract generalization that still has full concrete grounding.

---

### Level 4 — Prose Principle Lag (Implicit to Explicit)
**Theory:** Binswanger's observation that we use information before we conceptualize it — speaking prose without knowing grammar.

**Detection signal:** The human gives a "weird" or counter-intuitive instruction the AI resists. The human persists. Shortly after, they formulate a definition or principle for what they were doing.

**The climax:** The human moves from *doing* a new pattern to *naming* that pattern. This is the moment implicit grasp becomes explicit concept.

**Shuttling connection:** The human is at the top of the upward shuttle before they have articulated what they found. They are operating on a concept that hasn't yet been brought back down into words.

---

### Level 5 — Premise Check Pivot (Metacognition)
**Theory:** Binswanger's emphasis that free will is "sovereign control over how one uses his own mind" — including monitoring one's own mental operations.

**Detection signal:** The human aborts a task and provides a *causal* explanation of why the previous path was wrong.

**Critical differentiation (three branches):**
| Branch | Signal | Classification |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| A (Sacred Fire) | "I was wrong because X logically depends on Y which I violated" — causal explanation naming the violated dependency | Genuine logical contradiction |
| B (Confusion) | "Let me rephrase" — bare abort, no explanation | Noise |
| C (Rationalization) | "This felt overly complex / the code smelled bad" — evaluative explanation, not causal | Branch C: Eclectic/Unprincipled middle |

**Rule:** The marker fires *only* when the pivot includes a causal explanation, not just an abort. Branch C is the hardest case — the vocabulary of self-correction without the substance.

---

### Level 6 — Concretization Flood (Validation Signal)
**Theory:** Peikoff's "flood of concretes" standard — if you can't instantly generate diverse examples of a principle, you possess only the words.

**Detection signal:** After a human states a new abstraction, they *spontaneously* apply it to domains not previously discussed — without the AI having suggested these applications.

> "Wait — if the Event-Bus is the axis, then the authentication handshake is also an Event... and so is the analytics ping... and the cache invalidation trigger..."

**Role in the framework:** This is the **completion signal**. Levels 1-5 are candidates. Level 6 is verification. If the Flood does not follow the abstraction within a few exchanges, downgrade the detection — the human may have found the vocabulary without completing the upward shuttle.

**Negative signal (rationalist fingerprint):** Human states abstraction → waits → AI generates instances → human confirms. They possess the map, not the road.

---

### Level 7 — Spiral Phase Consciousness (Meta-Awareness)
**Theory:** The Spiral Theory of Knowledge — knowledge develops through iterative ascending loops. Phase 1 (initial learning) → Phase 2 (forward movement) → Phase 3 (return with enrichment).

**Detection signal:** The human explicitly refuses to name a concept they are clearly working with.
> "I don't have a name for this yet, but there's a pattern here" — and continues working.

**What this detects:** The human knows they are in Phase 2 of a spiral loop — they haven't returned with enough enrichment to name the concept correctly. They are demonstrating *meta-spiral awareness*: knowledge of their own position within the knowledge development cycle.

**Why it's the highest level:** It requires holding the entire conceptual structure, the spiral process itself, and their current position within it — simultaneously.

**Contrast with Level 4:** Level 4 (Prose Principle Lag) is acting before naming — can be involuntary. Level 7 is *deliberately* restraining naming — fully volitional.

## Shuttling: The Validation Engine

Shuttling (Peikoff) is the atomic cognitive motion that all markers are species of. The mind must move continuously between:

**Upward (Induction):** Concrete → Principle
**Downward (Concretization):** Principle → Concrete → Flood

**The Self-Test (directly applicable to transcript analysis):**
> "What is the first thing the human is eager to look at to feel they've made a beginning?"

- **Rationalist:** Craves a definition on the mental screen. States the principle, waits for the AI to apply it.
- **Inductivist:** Craves a flood of perceptual concretes. Generates new instances the AI hadn't mentioned.

**The "Table" Standard:** A principle must be as real as the concept "table" — instantly evoking a flood without conscious search. If a constraint only fires when the exact hint pattern appears, the human possesses the rule, not the concept.

**Connection to the Spiral:** Each turn of the Spiral is made of individual shuttling acts. Phase 1 = one upward shuttle. Phase 3 = a downward shuttle that enriches the original concept because Phase 2 produced new concretes to bring back. The Spiral is shuttling at macro-scale.

## The Genus Method Applied to the DDF

### Meta-Genus of All Seven Markers
**Genus:** Acts of volitional conceptual integration
**CCD:** The degree to which the act is volitional (initiated by the human's reasoning, not the AI's suggestion) AND the scope of integration (how many previously disparate units does it unify)

**Three branches (sweeping the territory):**
1. **Branch A (Principled Positive):** Genuine identification of new axis; causal explanation; unit reduction; integration survives downstream validation; Concretization Flood follows
2. **Branch B (Principled Negative):** Human never performs any of these acts — purely reactive, always addressing the AI's specific suggestion
3. **Branch C (Eclectic Middle):** Human performs *surface imitations* — uses vocabulary of integration without achieving unit reduction or surviving downstream validation

Branch C is the Flame-Spotter's hardest detection problem across all markers.

### The Big Divides Check for Contextual Transfusion
When a human imports a concept from a different domain ("treat this like Supply and Demand"), validate using:
1. **Structural isomorphism:** Does the imported axis capture a real causal commonality?
2. **Bidirectional enrichment (Spiral test):** Does applying the analogy make the source domain clearer too? Valid transfusions illuminate both directions.
3. **Downstream contradiction test:** If the imported framework generates 3+ contradictions downstream, it's category error with style — not Contextual Transfusion.

## Ordering: Hierarchy + Spiral (Two Axes)

The seven markers have two independent orderings:

**Vertical axis (Hierarchy / first-encounter order):**
Levels 1 → 7 represent increasing epistemological complexity. A human must have operated at Level 2 many times before Level 5 becomes possible.

**Horizontal axis (Spiral / retroactive enrichment):**
Each high-level detection event triggers a **backward pass** over prior transcript. A Level 5 Premise Check Pivot makes earlier Level 1-2 occurrences sharper in retrospect — you see the conceptual through-line you missed on the first pass.

**The Flame-Spotter agent therefore has two modes:**
- Forward scan (detecting markers as they occur)
- Backward enrichment (re-scoring earlier detections when a high-level marker fires)

## The Vertical Dimension: Structural Integrity Detection
## (Binswanger's Suspension Bridge, Chapter 6)

*Discovered: 2026-02-21 — the downward path and structural integrity signals that complete the DDF*

### The Limitation of the Seven Markers

The seven DDF markers detect the **upward path** — induction from concretes to principles. They answer: "Is the human ascending to genuine abstraction?"

They do not detect whether the resulting structure holds together. A session can have high Ignition activity (many Level 2-5 events) while producing floating abstractions, ungrounded principles, violated hierarchical dependencies, and no spiral reinforcement. The knowledge produced would look impressive and collapse under load.

Binswanger's Suspension Bridge (Chapter 6) adds the **downward path and structural integrity**: principle → concrete (reduction, validation, stabilization). This is the Bridge-Warden mode of the Flame-Spotter.

### Why the Bridge, Not the Skyscraper

The skyscraper analogy fails because it implies purely one-way, passive stacking. The bridge captures what the skyscraper cannot:

- **The earth** (perceptual reality) is the irreplaceable ground — every element must connect to it
- **The rungs and deck** (hierarchical levels) have a necessary order — you cannot skip
- **The main cables** (principles) actively hold up the rungs beneath them — higher-level abstractions stabilize lower-level structures by connecting them under unified tension
- **The integrated whole** — later knowledge enriches earlier knowledge; the roof strengthens the walls by connecting them

A principle does not passively sit atop its instances. It exerts downward tension that stabilizes, connects, and constrains them. This is the structural fact that makes the Suspension Bridge the correct analogy.

### The Four Structural Integrity Signals

**Signal A — The Gravity Check (Reduction)**
*Theory:* Every part of the bridge must connect to the earth. A disconnected element is a floating abstraction — cognitively weightless and structurally useless.

*Detection:* The human traces a high-level abstraction or error back to its perceptual ground. The AI proposes an abstract design pattern; the human says "this doesn't connect to the actual data we're processing — bring it back to what we're specifically handling."

*What it detects:* The human performing Reduction — pulling a floating element back to earth. Distinct from Level 2 (which moves upward from piles). The Gravity Check moves downward to verify the ground connection is intact.

*Bridge metaphor:* Discovering a span that has lost contact with its tower. The human reconnects it before the deck collapses.

---

**Signal B — The Main Cable (Top-Down Integration)**
*Theory:* In a suspension bridge, the main cables hold up the rungs beneath them. Strengthening the cable stabilizes the entire span.

*Detection:* The human strings a principle that holds up many existing concretes simultaneously — not just identifying the fundamental (Level 3) but deploying it downward to reorganize what already exists.

*Example:* "Every function must be idempotent." One statement is now a cable under tension across 1,000 lines of existing code. Previously unrelated functions are suddenly connected and stabilized under a single principle.

*What it detects:* The most dramatic unit-economy event — not just piles-to-files, but existing files suspended from a single cable. The Flame-Spotter measures the ratio: one principle, how many derivative structures simultaneously stabilized.

*CTT connection:* Op-8 (Top-Down Tension) — a principle is structurally operative only if it actively constrains its instances. If everything still passes after the principle is stated, it is a cable not bearing load.

---

**Signal C — Dependency Sequencing**
*Theory:* Hierarchy is the necessary order of learning. You cannot have the second span without the first tower. This is an epistemological constraint, not a project management preference.

*Detection:* The AI proposes building a high-level feature. The human refuses: "We can't build the dashboard until the data schema is finalized — the dashboard has no ground yet." The refusal is principled, not arbitrary.

*What it detects:* The human enforcing epistemological hierarchy in real time — acting as the Architect of Dependencies. They understand that the second span literally cannot stand without the first tower being logically grounded.

*Distinction from confusion:* The human must be able to state the logical dependency, not just express discomfort with the ordering. "The dashboard depends on the schema because every widget queries schema-defined fields" is Signal C. "I'm not comfortable building the dashboard yet" is noise.

---

**Signal D — Spiral Reinforcement**
*Theory:* Hierarchically later knowledge enriches prior knowledge — the roof adds stability to the walls by connecting them. This is the bridge's version of the Spiral Theory.

*Detection:* After building higher-level understanding, the human goes back and simplifies earlier code: "Now that we have the API handler working, we can make the data parser much leaner."

*What it detects:* The human using height they've achieved to repair and strengthen what's below. The integrated whole in operation — the human sees the bridge as a unified structure, not a linear list of tasks.

*Distinction from Level 7 (Spiral Phase Consciousness):* Level 7 is deliberate waiting — refusing to name a concept that isn't yet formed. Signal D is active deployment — using higher knowledge to act on lower knowledge. Level 7 is patient. Signal D is productive.

### The Two Modes of the Flame-Spotter

| Mode | What It Detects | Markers | Direction |
|------|----------------|---------|-----------|
| **Ignition Detection** (Phase 15) | Upward integration — new concepts forming | Levels 1-7 (FlameEvents) | Concrete → Principle |
| **Bridge-Warden** (Phase 17) | Structural integrity — knowledge structure sound | Signals A-D (StructuralEvents) | Principle → Concrete |

A complete session analysis requires both. High Ignition with poor Integrity = brilliant insights that float and collapse. High Integrity with low Ignition = solid structure but no conceptual advance. The ideal: ascending Ignition sustained by structural integrity at every level.

### CTT Extension: Op-8 (Top-Down Tension)

The bridge analogy reveals a gap in the CTT specification: we had the Flood Criterion (bottom-up validation — can the concept generate instances?) but no top-down criterion.

**Op-8: Top-Down Tension**
A principle P = ∀x : K . R(x) is structurally operative if and only if:
1. P eliminates at least one class of instances that would otherwise be valid members of K (it constrains, not just describes)
2. P connects previously independent instances of K under a common structural requirement (it integrates, not just labels)
3. Violations of P are detectable without reference to P's wording (the constraint is real, not definitional)

A principle that fails Op-8 is a floating cable — stated but not load-bearing. Every Main Cable detection event (Signal B) should trigger an Op-8 validation.

## The Memory Problem: Measurement Omission as the Unifying Theory

*Discovered: 2026-02-20 — the formal connection between Objectivist epistemology and the agent memory problem*

### The Crow Problem and the Human Solution

The human mind's Crow limit (roughly 7 units in focal awareness simultaneously) creates a fundamental dilemma for complex cognition: 10,000 lines of code produce Crow overload. You see a pile, not a file. You cannot think because you cannot hold the data.

Rand's solution — the mathematical core of concept formation — is **measurement omission**:

1. **Identify the CCD** (the axis all instances share but in different magnitudes)
2. **Omit the specific magnitudes** (retain the characteristic, drop the measurements)
3. **Assign a word** as the sensuous handle that makes the file portable
4. **Define scope** via the fundamental characteristic — the rule of membership

Result: 1,000,000 tables compressed into one mental unit. The Crow count drops from a million to one. Crucially, the specific tables are not lost — the concept gives instant access to any instance on demand (the flood test proves this).

As Rand states: *"The relationship of concepts to their constituent particulars is the same as the relationship of algebraic symbols to numbers... conceptual awareness is the algebra of cognition."*

### The Agent Memory Problem is the Same Problem

Current agent memory approaches all fail at the same point: **they store magnitudes, not CCDs.**

- RAG retrieval matches surface similarity — magnitude against magnitude. It retrieves the episode where the same words appeared, not the episode where the same axis was at play.
- MEMORY.md stores specific corrections and decisions — magnitudes. When a new situation has different surface features, the memory doesn't fire.
- Constraint stores record the text of corrections — magnitudes. A constraint recorded as "don't push to main without approval" fires only when those exact words and paths appear.

The result is the amnesia problem documented in Phase 10: the constraint was honored in text but violated in principle. The agent wasn't storing the concept — it was storing one instance of the concept.

### The DDF as Measurement Omission for Agent Memory

The DDF framework is the implementation of measurement omission for agent memory:

| Human Cognition | DDF / Agent Memory |
|---|---|
| CCD identified across instances | FlameEvent Level 2 — axis named |
| Specific magnitudes omitted | Raw JSONL stays on disk, not in active memory |
| Word assigned as portable handle | `axis_identified` field in FlameEvent |
| Definition sets membership rule | Constraint `detection_hints` — what counts as an instance |
| Flood test validates the concept is real | Level 6 Concretization Flood confirms before committing to memory |
| Spiral returns enrich the concept | Cross-session spiral tracking expands scope_paths over time |

**The FlameEvent store is not a memory bank — it is a concept store.** It stores CCDs with their scope rules and validation evidence. When a new session starts, the agent loads the conceptual structure — the axes identified, the scope rules governing them, the open questions at Level 7 not yet resolved — not raw past context.

### Amnesia Reframed

Decision amnesia (Phase 10) is not "the constraint was forgotten." It is "the constraint was stored at the wrong level of abstraction." A magnitude-level constraint has a narrow firing condition. A CCD-level constraint fires across any instance that shares the axis, regardless of surface form.

**The fix:** Store the CCD, omit the magnitudes, validate with the flood test. Detect amnesia not by checking whether the constraint text was honored, but whether the *principle* the constraint encodes was honored — which requires knowing what CCD generated the constraint in the first place.

This is the Phase 15 design principle: `epistemological_origin` on every constraint is not metadata — it determines how amnesia detection works. A `principled` constraint (generated from an O_AXS event with a named CCD) should be checked at the CCD level. A `reactive` constraint (generated from a single correction) should be checked at the magnitude level until enough instances promote it to CCD status.

### Why MEMORY.md Works When It Has the Right Content

MEMORY.md succeeds when it contains genuine Level 3+ FlameEvents — CCD-level insights expressed as scope rules. "DuckDB upsert requires staging table pattern" is a concept: it omits the specific magnitudes (which table, which session, which schema) and retains the CCD (the upsert mechanism requires staging). Any new DuckDB upsert situation triggers it regardless of surface differences.

MEMORY.md fails when it contains Level 1 noise — magnitudes stored as if they were CCDs. "Remember to check the schema" is a magnitude. It gives no rule of membership, no axis, no ability to fire in novel situations.

The DDF automates what MEMORY.md requires manual curation to achieve.

## The Human-AI Mirror Effect

*Discovered: 2026-02-20*

The apparent intelligence of an AI in a given session is not purely a property of the AI. It is a property of the **human-AI system** — co-created by both parties.

**The mechanism:** The human's operating level (which DDF marker they habitually issue) determines what level of reasoning the AI must perform to respond usefully. A Level 5 human question (Premise Check Pivot with causal explanation) elicits Level 5 reasoning. A Level 1 human question ("fix this button") elicits Level 1 reasoning.

This is not the AI adapting — it is the mathematical consequence of the conversation structure. The human sets the abstraction ceiling. The AI performs at that ceiling.

**Implications for the IntelligenceProfile (Phase 15):**

The profile is not measuring the human in isolation. It is measuring the human-AI system. Two humans with the same raw intelligence will produce different profiles if one habitually operates at Level 2 and the other at Level 5.

This means the IntelligenceProfile has a second use beyond measurement: **calibration of the AI's operating mode**. If the system knows a given human operates at Level 4-5, it can prime the AI to respond at that level rather than defaulting to Level 1-2 responses. The AI becomes a mirror that knows in advance what quality of reflection is expected.

**Implications for the candidate assessment (Phase 16):**

The scenarios must be designed to *allow* high-level operation, not just test for it. A scenario that only admits Level 1-2 solutions will produce Level 1-2 responses from even a Level 5 thinker. The calibration of pile problems to DDF levels (noted in the Phase 16 roadmap entry) is not optional — it is the core validity requirement of the assessment.

**Implications for signal density:**

Coding sessions have a high mechanical-to-conceptual ratio — tool calls, file reads, test runs, schema migrations. DDF markers are present but buried in operational noise. Philosophical or architectural discussions have almost no mechanical content; the markers are dense and visible. The Flame-Spotter agent must calibrate its sensitivity to session type, not apply a fixed threshold.

## Key Insight: What the System Measures

The Flame-Spotter is not measuring code quality, productivity, or correctness. It is measuring **identification acts** — moments where a human made something exist conceptually that did not exist before prompt N.

The question it is always asking:

> "Did the human make something exist conceptually that didn't exist before?"

That is a fundamentally different north star than any existing metric in software engineering assessment.

A secondary question, discovered through the memory connection:

> "Was the concept stored at the right level of abstraction — CCD or magnitude?"

The first question detects the Sacred Fire. The second determines whether it survives into the next session.

## Section 9: The Memory Architecture Substrate
## (Binswanger's Crow, Raven, and Fringe — Logistics of the Sacred Fire)

*Discovered: 2026-02-21 — the three-tier transport system that explains WHY all DDF markers are visible and WHY agent amnesia is structurally inevitable under current approaches*

---

### The Three-Tier Map

Every DDF marker is, in its essence, a **logistical event in a three-tier cognitive transport system**. The prior sections of this document answered *what to detect*. This section answers *why those signals are visible at all* and *what produces them*.

| Tier | Binswanger Term | OPE Analogy | Capacity | Access Cost |
|------|-----------------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| **Basement** | Long-term Memory / The Raven | Infinite JSONL archive | Unlimited | High — activation energy required; distant nodes cost more |
| **Library** | Working Memory / Activated Category | Loaded context window | Medium — one "shelf" | Low — shelf is already loaded |
| **Desktop** | Focal Awareness / The Crow | Active reasoning unit | 5-7 units | Zero — already in focus |

**The Raven** is the limit on retrievability. You cannot activate your entire brain at once. You have a fixed budget of activation energy to pull items from the Basement into the Library. The further the concept from your current focus (measured in conceptual "nodes"), the higher the energy cost.

**The Crow** is the limit on focal capacity (Miller's Law — roughly 7 units simultaneously). Once the Desktop is full, new items cannot be processed without filing existing ones back into the Library.

**The Fringe** is the region between Basement and Desktop — peripheral awareness. Things you are "sort of" aware of but have not named. Fringe contents can drift back to Basement (insight loss) or ascend to Desktop (Level 4: Prose Principle Lag, when they are finally named).

---

### Every DDF Marker Is a Tier-Shift Event

| Level | DDF Marker | Memory Move | What the Flame-Spotter Actually Sees |
|-------|-----------|-------------|--------------------------------------|
| **0** | Trunk Identification | Basement re-indexed | Human rejects AI's superficial retrieval and names the filing key — restructures the entire Basement around a fundamental |
| **1** | Algebra of the Prompt | Desktop: constants → variable | Crow overload (20 bug lines) collapses to 1 unit ("Race Condition") |
| **2** | Piles to Files (Axis Shift) | Library reorganization | Human clears one shelf, loads a new one with a different framing |
| **3** | Causal Isolation | Basement → Desktop direct | Distal Retrieval — pulls a fundamental from a far node (Law of Identity vs. a React bug) |
| **4** | Prose Principle Lag | Fringe → Library → Desktop | "Something feels off" (Fringe) eventually becomes "X is the cause" (Desktop) |
| **5** | Premise Check Pivot | Desktop audit + Basement correction | Human audits Desktop, identifies wrong Library shelf, returns to Basement for re-retrieval |
| **6** | Concretization Flood | Desktop → Library (mass filing) | Automatization — abstraction stored on the shelf so it no longer requires Desktop attention |
| **7** | Spiral Phase Consciousness | Deliberate Library → Desktop suspension | Human *knows* the axis is identified but deliberately refuses to load instances until more Basement retrieval enriches the Library |

**Level 0 — Trunk Identification** (newly identified, 2026-02-21):

*Theory:* To beat the Raven, file by essentials. Fundamentals are the trunk of a causal tree. Know the trunk and you have a direct path to every leaf. File by superficials (lollipops and stripes — surface features rather than causal properties) and the Raven must wander through 35 nodes to find any given leaf.

*Detection signal:* The AI proposes a fix based on superficial similarity ("this looks like a previous UI bug"). The human rejects it, naming the fundamental: "No — the root is the **Data Ownership model**." By naming the trunk, the human re-indexes the entire Basement around a causal filing system.

*Why it is Level 0:* Without Trunk Identification, all 7 markers above it are working from a mis-indexed Basement. The Raven will retrieve from the wrong node. False positives will proliferate at Levels 1-6. The Trunk is the prerequisite for everything else — not a higher-level marker, but a ground-level one.

*Bridge connection:* Trunk Identification is Signal B (Main Cable) in its Basement-phase form — the human is not yet deploying the principle downward onto existing concretes, but is establishing the cable's anchor point. Signal B fires when the cable is strung. Level 0 fires when the anchor is identified.

### The AI's Cognitive Profile in This Model

The Crow/Raven model makes the AI's cognitive architecture formally precise — and reveals a structural asymmetry at the heart of all human-AI collaboration:

| Cognitive Component | Human | AI |
|---|---|---|
| **Basement (Long-term)** | Vast but bounded; personal life + professional experience | Effectively infinite; all training data |
| **Raven (Retrieval Cost)** | Real — distant nodes cost activation energy | **None** — retrieves with equal ease from any distance |
| **Library (Working Memory)** | Medium capacity; loads one shelf at a time | Context window — loaded once per session |
| **Crow (Focal Awareness)** | 5-7 units; applies selection pressure | **None** — no selective focus; processes everything in context |
| **Fringe** | Real — peripheral awareness drives Level 4 | **None** — no pre-conceptual awareness |

**The critical asymmetry:** The AI has an **infinite Basement but no Crow and no Raven**. This is not a limitation that will be fixed by scaling — it is a structural feature of the architecture.

- **No Crow** means the AI cannot essentialization. It cannot reduce 100 inputs to 7 because it does not experience Crow overload. Every item is equally in focus (or none is).
- **No Raven** means the AI has no motivation to file by essentials. The Raven's cost function is what drives Trunk Identification in humans — because it costs energy to reach distant nodes, you learn to index the Basement by fundamentals. Without cost, there is no selection pressure for concept formation.

**The collaboration equation:**

> *Human Crow × AI Basement = the Sacred Fire*

The human provides focal selection (Crow). The AI provides unlimited retrieval (Basement). The DDF detects the moments when the human successfully uses their Crow to direct the AI's Basement retrieval toward the Essential — and away from the infinite noise of superficially similar magnitudes.

**The "broken Desktop" anti-pattern:** When the AI's Basement output overwhelms the human's Crow (too many options, no Trunk), the human enters symptom-chasing mode. They process the AI's magnitude output without performing Trunk Identification. This is the precursor state to high DDF markers — a sustained Crow overload that forces the human to eventually perform Level 0-3 to survive. The Flame-Spotter should recognize this pattern: extended Crow overload followed by Trunk Identification is the classic DDF arc.

This is a new real-time intervention type distinct from the O_AXS intervention (which fires after the axis is named). The **Fringe Intervention** fires *before* naming, attempting to accelerate the Fringe→Focus transition and prevent Drift.
